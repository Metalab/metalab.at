Metalab

Metalab is a space where you can pursue your curiosity, realize unique ideas or simply meet and exchange with others. It offers an atmosphere in which all kinds of people interested in art, technology, society and sciences come together to share knowledge and work on their personal and collaborative projects.

What Do We Do?

Programming, robotics, DIY, amateur radio, electronic music, analog photography, art installations, visual and other art, upycling, sewing, karaoke, sign language, food hacking.

What is a Hackspace?

Before Hollywood hijacked the term hacking for non-qualitative action movies, hacking meant changing the function of an object to do something else than it was intended to. So hacking isn't limited to software programming or tinkering with electronics - it can also mean to make your own functional clothing or building a music instrument out of mayonnaise or anything really!

It can be thought of being more related to the phrase ‚Äúlife hack‚Äù. A hacker is a person who uses their knowledge and curiosity to creatively overcome challenges in their speciality, be it Information Technology, or any other field.

Tools and Infrastructure

We have a large selection of tools and machines, including a laser cutter, 3d printers, a CNC mill, lathe, sewing machines, a vinyl plotter, soldering irons, and oscilloscopes, all of which can also be used by guests, but may require supervision. If you want to use the 3D printer, CNC, or laser cutter, please contact core@metalab.at to find a date where someone can supervise/help you.

Visit Us!

Metalab is generally open every day after 6pm, and often earlier if at least one member is in the space.

For your first visit, we recommend coming during one of our regular open days (held on the second Thursday and fourth Friday of each month). During the open day, there will always be someone happy to give you a tour of the space and people helping you use our machines. Alternatively, you can also visit MetaInvasion day, where the space is open exclusively to FINTA* people.
